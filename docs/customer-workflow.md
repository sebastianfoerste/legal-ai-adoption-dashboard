# Customer workflow

How this dashboard is used across the life of an account, from onboarding to renewal.

## Onboarding (weeks 0–2)
Seats are provisioned and the account appears with a thin trend and a low score. That is
expected. The job here is baseline: which practice groups are live, which personas hold
seats, who the Innovation lead is.

## Activation (weeks 2–8)
The weekly-active trend is the signal that matters. A group whose bars climb is
self-sustaining; a flat or falling group needs intervention. Blockers get logged as they
surface — a partner who will not trust output without a citation is a `trust_in_output`
blocker, not a lost cause.

## Quarterly business review
Open Account Health live with the customer. Walk the health badge, then the blockers with
their attached re-engagement actions, then the feedback queue. The QBR stops being a
status read-out and becomes a working session: book the workshop, confirm the integration
pilot, show that their feedback reached Product.

## Re-engagement (any time the score drops)
A falling trend or a new high-severity blocker moves an account into "needs attention" on
the Blockers page. The attached action is the playbook: pair a skeptic with the Innovation
lead, run the partner briefing on review gates, mandate the associate hands-on.

## Expansion and renewal
An account reads "Expansion-ready" only when it is healthy, well-utilized, and growing.
That flag is the trigger for the expansion conversation — earned on evidence, not pushed
on a calendar. Renewal then argues from momentum: trend up, blockers closing, feedback
shipping.

Throughout: the product gates every output behind a named lawyer. This dashboard never
actions an expansion or renewal automatically — it surfaces the signal for a human to act on.
